## II. Scenario-Based Assessment

### Scenario 1: Problem-Solving & Creativity

**Prompt:** You're given a small budget to improve community engagement in a neighborhood that's been feeling disconnected. You can't host big events or spend much money. What would you do to bring people together?

**Response:** It would depend on exactly how much of a budget I was given as well as the size of the community. My first thought is personally seek out the largest outdoor park and speaking with the department in charge to secure the ability to host a community BBQ. Once that's completed I would create flyers and go door to door handing them out.

---

### Scenario 2: Emotional Intelligence & Empathy

**Prompt:** A close friend confides in you that they feel like a failure because they haven't achieved their career goals. They're clearly upset and withdrawn. How do you respond, and what do you say to support them?

**Response:** I can honestly count the number of people I would genuinely consider a friend on one hand. I've had these types of discussions with those few I do consider a friend and if I don't already know their career goals then I'll ask, and then ask what they feel they would need to improve in order to achieve that goal. It may not always be the most compassionate thing to do but I usually try to get them to think about from a more analytical perspective.

---

### Scenario 3: Strategic Thinking & Decision-Making

**Prompt:** You're leading a team on a project with a tight deadline. Halfway through, you realize the original plan isn't working. You have two options: stick with the plan and hope for the best, or pivot and risk missing the deadline. What do you do, and how do you explain your decision to your team?

**Response:** I've said hope for the best but expect the worst, but that's only when I feel I have analyzed every possible scenario and the potential outcomes. So if halfway through the project I know for certain it will not work then I would explain exactly why I believe it won't work and ask their opinion. I feel that it's best to ask everyone involved in the project before making a final decision because they just might have an idea that fixes everything.
